\section*{Ethics Statement}
\label{sec:ethics}

This work uses the EFCAMDAT learner corpus under its published research
licence \citep{geertzen2014efcamdat}, and the CEFR-classification
derivative splits used for transfer evaluation have been prepared as
part of the first author's PhD research programme.

Learner data is sensitive: misuse of proficiency- or L1-conditioned
generation could reinforce stereotypes about how speakers of particular
L1s ``should'' write in English, or produce mock-learner text that
misrepresents authentic learners. We intend the gated model as a
research instrument for cognitive-modelling and diagnostic
applications (intelligent tutoring, proficiency-calibrated material
generation), not as a public-facing generator. Any downstream
deployment would need (i) explicit consent from the learner whose
metadata is used, (ii) guardrails that prevent the generation of
artificial learner output from being passed off as human learner data
in corpus work, and (iii) a fallback path for learners whose metadata
is incomplete or whose L1 is not represented in the training inventory.

We note also that continual pre-training on EFCAMDAT does not anonymise
the corpus further than its released form already does. Members of
research teams with access to the underlying identifiers should not
use the trained model in ways that could re-identify individual
learners.
